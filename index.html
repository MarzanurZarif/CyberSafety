Cyber Security

Cybersecurity means protecting computers, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, and damage. It is the practice of protecting internet-connected systems, networks, software, and data from digital attacks. Just like you lock your house, cybersecurity locks your digital life.

We depend on the internet every day — for study, banking, shopping, and entertainment. Without proper cybersecurity, anyone can fall victim to hacking or data theft.

Cyber Attacks

Common cyber attacks we fall for

Viruses & Malware

Programs that enter your device without permission and can damage files, slow down your system, or steal data.
Example: A crack download that secretly installs harmful software.

Cyber Attack Virus Malware
Phishing

Fake emails or messages that look real and try to trick you into revealing passwords or clicking dangerous links.
Example: Your bank account is locked. Click here to verify. Lottery winning site.

Cyber Attack Links Steal
Ransomware

A type of malware that locks your files and demands money to unlock them.
Example: Hackers encrypt company data and ask for payment to restore it.

Cyber Attack Money Ransome
Social Engineering

When someone manipulates you into giving personal information or doing something unsafe.
Example: A “friend” asking for your OTP code.

Cyber Attack Scam Calls OTP
Deepfake Attacks

Utilize AI to create realistic fake audio or video to trick victims into taking malicious actions.
Example: Your close ones voice cloned and asking for money.

Cyber Attack AI Clone
Spyware

Secretly monitors user activity to collect sensitive data, such as passwords or credit card information.
Example: Key logger secretly capturing all keystrokes on a user's device.

Cyber Attack Record Spy

2 Factor Authentication

Extra security layer by requiring a code or approval after your password. Even if someone steals your password, they can’t access your account without the second factor.

Cyber Safety 2FA Confirm
Avoid Suspicious Links

Never click links from emails, texts, or DMs that look suspicious or too good to be true. Always check the sender’s address and website URL before visiting a site.
